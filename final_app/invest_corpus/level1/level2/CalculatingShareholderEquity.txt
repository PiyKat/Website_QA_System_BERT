 Calculating Shareholder Equity . The balance sheet holds the basis of the accounting equation: Calculating Shareholder Equity . Shareholder equity can also be expressed as a company's share capital and retained earnings less the value of treasury shares. This method, however, is less common. Though both methods yield the same figure, the use of total assets and total liabilities is more illustrative of a company's financial health. By comparing concrete numbers reflecting everything the company owns and everything it owes, the "assets-minus-liabilities" shareholder equity equation paints a clear picture of a company's finances that is easily interpreted by investors, professionals, and laypersons.
