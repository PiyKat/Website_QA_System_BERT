 What is an Interest Rate Swap? . An interest rate swap is a forward contract in which one stream of future interest payments is exchanged for another based on a specified principal amount. Interest rate swaps usually involve the exchange of a fixed interest rate for a floating rate, or vice versa, to reduce or increase exposure to fluctuations in interest rates or to obtain a marginally lower interest rate than would have been possible without the swap. What is an Interest Rate Swap? . A swap can also involve the exchange of one type of floating rate for another, which is called a basis swap.
