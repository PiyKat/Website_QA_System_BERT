 Vega and Vomma . Vomma is a second-order Greek derivative which means that its value provides insight on how Vega will change with an implied volatility of the underlying instrument. If a positive vomma is calculated and volatility increases, vega on the option position will increase. If volatility falls, a positive vomma would indicate a decrease in vega. If vomma is negative, the opposite occurs with volatility changes as indicated by vega’s convexity. Vega and Vomma .
