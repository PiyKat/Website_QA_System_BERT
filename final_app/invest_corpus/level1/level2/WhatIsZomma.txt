 What Is Zomma? . Zomma is a measure of the degree to which the gamma of a derivative is sensitive to changes in implied volatility. It is also referred to as DgammaDvol. What Is Zomma? . It is part of a category of measurements used to assess the price sensitivity of a derivative to various factors, such as changes in interest rates, volatility, or the spot price of the derivative's underlying asset. These measurements are commonly referred to as "Greeks" because they are denoted by Greek symbols.
