ROE vs. Return on Invested Capital . The purpose of ROIC is to figure out the amount of money after dividends a company makes based on all its sources of capital, which includes shareholders equity and debt. ROE looks at how well a company utilizes shareholder equity, while ROIC is meant to determine how well a company uses all its available capital to make money.
