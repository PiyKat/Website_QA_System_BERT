 Dual Mandate . The Federal Reserve’s main monetary policymaking body is the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC), which includes the Board of Governors, president of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, and presidents of four other regional Federal Reserve Banks who serve on a rotating basis. Dual Mandate . The committee is responsible for monetary policy decisions, which are categorized into three areas; maximizing employment, stabilizing prices and moderating long-term interest rates. The first two are know as the Fed's dual mandate.
