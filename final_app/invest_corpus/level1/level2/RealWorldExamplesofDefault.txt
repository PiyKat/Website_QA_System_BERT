Long-Term Capital Management was a massive hedge fund that defaulted and eventually closed its doors in 2000. Business Insider reports how the fund's exposure to Brazilian, Danish and Russia bonds and other risky investment spun out of control when Russia defaulted on its sovereign bonds. Long-Term Capital lost over $4 billion in a few months, and even with the Federal Reserve's attempts to save it, the hedge fund eventually went bankrupt. Long-Term Capital was the first hedge fund in U.S. history to go bankrupt and close its doors.
