 What Is a Margin Call? . A margin call occurs when the value of an investor's margin account (that is, one that contains securities bought with borrowed money) falls below the broker's required amount. A margin call is the broker's demand that an investor deposit additional money or securities so that the account is brought up to the minimum value, known as the maintenance margin. What Is a Margin Call? . A margin call usually means that one or more of the securities held in the margin account has decreased in value below a certain point. The investor must either deposit more money in the account or sell some of the assets held in the account.
