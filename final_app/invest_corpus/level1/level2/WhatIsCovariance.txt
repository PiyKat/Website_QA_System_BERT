 What Is Covariance? . The fields of mathematics and statistics offer a great many tools to help us evaluate stocks. One of these is covariance, which is a statistical measure of the directional relationship between two asset prices. One may apply the concept of covariance to anything, but here the variables are stock prices. Formulas that calculate covariance can predict how two stocks might perform relative to each other in the future. Applied to historical prices, covariance can help determine if stocks' prices tend to move with or against each other. What Is Covariance? . Using the covariance tool, investors might even be able to select stocks that complement each other in terms of price movement.
