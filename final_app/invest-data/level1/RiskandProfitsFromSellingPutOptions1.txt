 Risk and Profits From Selling Put Options . A call option buyer has the right to buy assets at a price that is lower than the market when the stock's price is rising. The put option buyer can profit by selling stock at the strike price when the market price is below the strike price. Option sellers receive a premium fee from the buyer for writing an option. In a falling market, the put option seller may be forced to buy the asset at the higher strike price than they would normally pay in the market The call option writer faces infinite risk if the stock's price rises significantly and they are forced to buy shares at a high price. Option buyers must pay an upfront premium to the writers of the option.
