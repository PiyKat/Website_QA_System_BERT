 Taking Bets . One of the often-proclaimed benefits of trading S&P 500 futures is each contract represents an immediate, indirect investment in the performance of the 500 stocks in the S&P 500 Index. Investors can take long or short positions depending on their expectations for future prices. Large institutions may use S&P 500 futures to hedge positions in the S&P 500 Index. With this approach, futures are often used to offset downside risks.
