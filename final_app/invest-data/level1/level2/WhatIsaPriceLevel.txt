 What Is a Price Level? . A price level is the average of current prices across the entire spectrum of goods and services produced in the economy. In more general terms, price level refers to the price or cost of a good, service, or security in the economy. Price levels may be expressed in small ranges, such as ticks with securities prices, or presented as a discrete value such as a dollar figure. In economics, price levels are a key indicator and are closely watched by economists. They play an important role in the purchasing power of consumers as well as the sale of goods and services. it also plays an important part in the supply-demand chain.
