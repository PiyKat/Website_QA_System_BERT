 How the Troubled Asset Relief Program (TARP) Worked . Global credit markets came to a near standstill in September 2008, as several major financial institutions, such as Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, and American International Group (AIG), experienced severe financial problems, and others, like Lehman Brothers, went bankrupt—the effects of the subprime mortgage crisis that had begun the previous year. Investment companies Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley changed their charters to become commercial banks, in an attempt to stabilize their capital situations.
