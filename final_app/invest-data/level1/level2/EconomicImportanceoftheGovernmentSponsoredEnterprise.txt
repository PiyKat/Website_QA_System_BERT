 Economic Importance of the Government-Sponsored Enterprise . Their aggregate loans in the secondary market make GSEs the largest financial institutions in the U.S. A collapse of even one GSE could lead to a downward spiral in the markets, which could lead to an economic disaster. Since they have an implicit guarantee from the government that they will not be allowed to fail, GSEs are considered by critics to be stealth recipients of corporate welfare. In fact, following the 2008 subprime mortgage crisis, Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac received $187 billion worth of federal assistance almost immediately to mitigate the negative impact that the wave of defaults was wrecking on the housing market and the national economy. They were also placed into government conservatorship. Both agencies have repaid their respective bailouts since then, though they remain under the control by the Federal Housing Finance Agency.
